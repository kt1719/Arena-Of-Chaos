# Skill: Elegance (Ch. 2)

## Definition
Elegance = maximum emotional power and variety from minimum comprehension burden and developer effort. Achieved through **emergence** — when simple mechanics interact to create complex situations.

## The Elegance Smells
Trained intuition for spotting elegant designs. Apply these as a checklist:

1. **Many Interactions** — Does this mechanic interact with many other mechanics? Count the connections. More = more elegant.
2. **Simplicity** — Can you write it on a cocktail napkin? Reducing complexity increases player appreciation of what's left.
3. **Multiple Uses** — Can it be used creatively, offensively, defensively, tactically, strategically? (Resident Evil: guns are both offense AND defense because shooting stops zombies in place)
4. **Non-Overlapping Roles** — Does each mechanic serve a unique purpose? If two mechanics do the same thing, one is dead weight.
5. **Reuses Conventions** — Does it leverage knowledge players already have from other games, real life, or cultural archetypes? Don't change conventions without good reason.
6. **Similar Scale** — Do the numbers work together? Magic: The Gathering's life (20), creature power (1-8), mana (3-10), cards in hand (7) all interact naturally. Mismatched scales break elegance.
7. **High Reuse** — Will this mechanic be used thousands of times? A mechanic used once is a gimmick. Elegance requires repetition that stays fresh through emergence.
8. **No Content Restrictions** — Does this mechanic force all future content into narrow constraints? Hidden costs that smother a game over time.
9. **Full Interface Expressiveness** — Does it use the full range of the input device? Analog sticks sense exact angles, not just directions. Triggers sense partial pulls.

## Case Study: Predator vs Hellion (StarCraft II)
Both are fast, mid-cost, area-of-effect units. Nearly identical stats. The difference:
- **Predator**: Circular damage around itself. Few tactical options. Just throw it in and watch.
- **Hellion**: Line-shaped flame attack. Creates an entire class of play: lining up shots, opponents trying to surround, shoot-and-scoot tactics, synergy with walls/ledges/other units.

The Hellion is no more complex to understand, but generates vastly more situations. **That's elegance.**

## Key Principle
Elegance doesn't come from flashy gimmicks or exciting pitches. It comes from simple, workmanlike designs that flower into a million experiences. The most elegant mechanics sound boring when described but are endlessly rich in play.

## Teaching Questions
- "How many other mechanics does this interact with? List them."
- "What happens if you remove this mechanic? What experiences are lost?"
- "Does this mechanic overlap with anything else in the design?"
- "What content restrictions does this create for future development?"
- "Can you describe this mechanic in one sentence?"
