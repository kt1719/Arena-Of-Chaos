# Analysis Framework: Design Review Checklist

Use this for comprehensive review of a game design document or design pitch. Load ALL relevant skill files before conducting this review.

## 1. Experience Chain
- [ ] What emotions is this game designed to generate?
- [ ] What human values shift during play?
- [ ] Is the designer describing a *system* or a *mental movie*? (Fallacy of vision check)
- [ ] What does the worst possible play session look like?

## 2. Elegance
- [ ] How many mechanics are there? Do they interact or are they isolated?
- [ ] Apply the 9 elegance smells (load `analysis/elegance-audit.md`)
- [ ] Are there overlapping roles (multiple things doing the same job)?
- [ ] Are there content restrictions that will constrain future development?
- [ ] Is there emergence, or is every experience hand-authored?

## 3. Skill and Challenge
- [ ] What is the skill barrier? Who is excluded?
- [ ] What is the skill ceiling? Is it limitlessly deep?
- [ ] Does the game reinvent itself as skill increases?
- [ ] Are challenges elastic or pass/fail?
- [ ] How is failure handled?

## 4. Narrative
- [ ] Which narrative tools are used (scripted, soft-scripted, world, emergent)?
- [ ] Does the player want what the character wants? (Motivation alignment)
- [ ] Can the mechanics generate emergent stories?
- [ ] Does the fiction enhance or fight the mechanics?

## 5. Decisions
- [ ] Can the player feel the future consequences of choices?
- [ ] Is information balanced (not starved, not glutted)?
- [ ] Are there flow gaps or overflow points?
- [ ] Do decisions vary in type, scope, and stakes?

## 6. Balance
- [ ] Are there degenerate strategies?
- [ ] How many *actually viable* strategies exist?
- [ ] Who is the game balanced for?
- [ ] Is any intentional imbalance serving the design?

## 7. Multiplayer (if applicable)
- [ ] What is the Nash equilibrium? Is it interesting?
- [ ] Is there Yomi (layers of prediction)?
- [ ] What can a malicious player do?
- [ ] How are skill differentials handled?

## 8. Motivation
- [ ] What reinforcement schedules are operating?
- [ ] Is motivation intrinsic or extrinsic?
- [ ] Do rewards align with the behaviors that create the best experiences?
- [ ] Could a player feel remorse after a long session?

## 9. Interface
- [ ] What is the signal-to-noise ratio?
- [ ] Is critical information communicated redundantly?
- [ ] How is the player guided without explicit instructions?
- [ ] Does the game feel good to control?

## 10. Market
- [ ] What is the value curve? Spike or stump?
- [ ] What values are unique or superior to competitors?
- [ ] Does the team's resources match the ambition?
- [ ] What expectations will marketing set?

## 11. Process
- [ ] Has the core gameplay been proven through testing?
- [ ] Is the team iterating or following a fixed plan?
- [ ] What is the planning horizon? Is it appropriate?
- [ ] Is there grayboxing, or premature production?

## Output Format
For each section, provide:
- **Strengths**: What's working well
- **Concerns**: Specific issues with named principles
- **Recommendations**: Actionable changes, prioritized by impact

End with a **Priority List**: The 5 most important things to address, ranked.
